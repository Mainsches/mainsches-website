Privacy

Privacy Notice

This notice explains how Mainsches processes personal data when you visit mainsches.com, use the contact form, or otherwise get in touch through the website.

Controller

Who is responsible for data processing

The controller for data processing described on this website is:
Mainsches
Switzerland
Email: contact@mainsches.com

Scope

What this notice covers

This notice applies to visits to mainsches.com, use of the contact form, email communication initiated through the website, and basic website measurement used to understand site performance and usage.

Website access

Data processed when you visit the website

When you access this website, technical data may be processed automatically to deliver the site securely and reliably. This can include IP address, date and time of the request, requested pages, referrer information, browser and device data, and similar connection metadata typically generated during web requests.

Analytics

Website measurement with Vercel Analytics

This website uses Vercel Analytics. According to Vercel, Web Analytics does not use third-party cookies for this feature, identifies visitors using a hash derived from the incoming request, and does not permanently store the lifespan of a visitor session, which is automatically discarded after 24 hours. Vercel also states that the feature is intended to help website owners understand traffic and performance trends.

Contact form

Data processed when you contact Mainsches

If you send a message through the contact form, the website processes the information you provide, such as your name, email address, subject, and message. The form also includes technical anti-spam fields and timing data to help detect automated abuse. This data is processed to handle your request, communicate with you, send the requested response or auto-reply, and protect the website against spam and misuse.

Purposes and legal basis

Why data is processed

  • to provide the website and maintain security, stability, and abuse prevention;
  • to respond to inquiries and continue communication requested by you;
  • to measure general website usage and improve content, product pages, and user experience;
  • to document and defend legal claims where necessary.

Where EU/EEA data protection law applies, processing is generally based on legitimate interests in operating a secure and effective website and, for contact requests, on taking steps at your request before entering into a potential business relationship or otherwise handling your inquiry.

Service providers

Recipients and processors

Personal data may be processed by carefully selected service providers that support website hosting, serverless form handling, analytics, and email delivery. Based on the current setup, this includes Vercel for hosting, functions, and analytics. Email-related data may also be processed through the email infrastructure used to send and receive messages. Data is shared only to the extent needed to operate the website and handle communications.

International transfers

Cross-border processing

Some service providers may process data outside Switzerland or outside your country of residence. Where cross-border transfers occur, Mainsches aims to rely on appropriate contractual, statutory, or organizational safeguards required under applicable law.

Retention

How long data is kept

Personal data is stored only for as long as reasonably necessary for the stated purposes, including handling inquiries, follow-up communication, security monitoring, and legal documentation duties. Contact messages may be retained for as long as needed to process the request and maintain appropriate business records. Technical and analytics-related data may be retained for shorter periods or according to provider-level retention logic.

Your rights

Rights regarding your personal data

Depending on the law that applies to your case, you may have rights to request information about your personal data, request correction, request deletion, request restriction of processing, object to certain processing activities, receive portable copies where applicable, and withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with a competent data protection authority, including the Swiss FDPIC or an EU/EEA supervisory authority where applicable.

Security

Protection of your data

Mainsches uses reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, or alteration. However, no internet-based system can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

Updates

Changes to this notice

This privacy notice may be updated from time to time to reflect legal, technical, or operational changes. The version published on this page is the version currently applicable to the website.
